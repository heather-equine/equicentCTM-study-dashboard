QUICK TALKING POINTS
Why choose eCTM?
A single cryopreserved allograft dose (thaw ~10 min, no blood draw, no processing) outperformed SOC biologics (PRP, IRAP, stem cells) in every measured outcome — pain, swelling, lameness, performance — with 4× fewer adverse events. No second visit required.
How fast does it work?
Statistically significant improvement by Week 2 for pain and swelling. By Week 12, 87–93% cumulative reductions in pain, swelling, and composite scores (p<0.003), sustained through 26 weeks.
Will horses actually become sound?
eCTM horses were 3× more likely to be completely pain-free (p<0.001) and 3.2× more likely to achieve zero joint pain (p<0.004) by 12 weeks. For lameness, eCTM horses were 1.6× more likely to achieve a zero lameness score (p≤0.06). These aren’t just improvements — they’re complete resolution of clinical signs.
Is it safe?
In the clinical trial (n=138), eCTM was 4× safer than standard of care overall and 8.3× safer for joints specifically (p<0.013). In the separate safety study, zero adverse events were observed in 8 normal horses at 42 days, and no systemic inflammation was detected — SAA levels dropped post-injection.
Does it actually repair tissue?
Yes — 36% of eCTM ligaments showed positive tissue remodeling on computational ultrasound vs 0% for SOC. eCTM is the only treatment that achieved both clinical improvement AND structural repair.
What about performance horses?
Cumulative performance index was +153% higher for eCTM (p<0.001). Return to prior performance: 2.72:1 odds. SOC joints declined after Week 12 while eCTM kept improving.
What makes the product different?
It's not just growth factors — it's a fetal matrix implant with 2,645 proteins released over time as it biodegrades, plus billions of regenerative extracellular vesicles. Native scaffold architecture is preserved.
Why is this landmark evidence?
This is the first controlled trial for any allograft MSK therapy in animals. It is the first and strongest level of evidence for an allograft musculoskeletal tissue therapy in animals, advancing the published evidence base for comparable human joint allografts and MSC retrospective clinical studies in dogs. Prospective, multicenter, randomized, controlled design — the highest level of clinical evidence short of blinding. 138 horses across 8 practices and 11 veterinarians in real-world conditions. Contemporaneous SOC controls included the best available biologic therapies (PRP, ACS, APS), not just saline. 26-week follow-up with near-complete retention (135 of 138 horses).
Why does eCTM work?
eCTM works through a triple-action mechanism from fetal umbilical tissue. First, it delivers abundant fetal anabolic, anti-catabolic, and anti-inflammatory proteins directly to the injury site. Second, biodegradable tissue microparticles slowly break down over time, providing sustained local release of growth factors and inhibitors for weeks — like a slow-release medication. Third, the fibrillar tissue scaffold supplies fetal collagens and glycans, acting as a framework the horse's own cells can grow into — supporting cellular ingrowth and compressive resistance. Prior in vitro studies confirmed microparticles adhere to inflamed synovium and gradually biodegrade while releasing IGF, GAGs, and TIMP-2.

Anti-Inflammatory Response
IL-1ra surge and favorable cytokine shift
FROM THE MANUSCRIPT

IL-1ra increased significantly (P<0.001) on Days 1, 3, and 10 after UC injection and were greater than saline control and baseline. The statistically significant increase of IL-1ra with UC administration resulted in an increase of the IL-1ra to IL-1 ratio several-fold greater than baseline and control (P<0.001). IL-10 was significantly greater (P<0.01) than both baseline and control after UC injection.

ⓘ WHAT THIS MEANS
When eCTM is injected into a joint, it triggers a powerful anti-inflammatory response. The body produces significantly more IL-1ra (a natural inflammation blocker) and IL-10 (an anti-inflammatory molecule). This protective shift lasts well beyond the initial injection response, suggesting the product actively helps protect joint cartilage from breakdown.
KEY POINTS
  • IL-1ra increased significantly (P<0.001) — greater than saline control and baseline
  • IL-1ra/IL-1 ratio increased several-fold, persisting beyond the transient synovitis
  • IL-10 significantly elevated (P<0.01) — anti-inflammatory and chondroprotective
  • Seven of eight horses maintained elevated IL-1ra for the full study duration

Mild, self-resolving response
FROM THE MANUSCRIPT

Synovial fluid showed a transient transudative synovitis after UC injection that was greater than baseline and control and returned to normal after Day 5 (P<0.001). Horses administered UC did not demonstrate clinical signs of lameness, pain, or inflammation after Day 1 for at least 21 days.

ⓘ WHAT THIS MEANS
After injection, there's a brief, mild fluid response in the joint that peaks on Day 1 and clears up within 5–10 days. Importantly, this did not cause any lameness or pain — the horses were comfortable throughout. This kind of mild response is completely normal and expected with any intra-articular injection.
KEY POINTS
  • Peaked on Day 1, returned to normal by Day 5–10
  • Zero clinical lameness or pain at any time point
  • Consistent response pattern across both first and second injections (21 days apart)
  • Typical of any intra-articular injection — resolved without treatment

Cryopreservation Advantage
Why frozen microparticles outperform alternatives
FROM THE MANUSCRIPT

Fresh allogeneic grafts offer the advantages of living tissue but carry the proven risk of disease transmission. Intact frozen allografts are subject to the effects of freezing tissue in bulk, in part due to limited penetration of cryoprotectants. The underlying quality of the product is assured by cryopreservation of fresh tissue, donor health and uniformity, and a meticulously selected tissue source of low immunogenicity.

ⓘ WHAT THIS MEANS
The microparticulate approach solves problems that other tissue products face. By breaking the tissue into tiny particles before freezing, the cryoprotectant can penetrate evenly, preserving protein integrity. The result is a cryopreserved allograft that's thawed and ready in about 10 minutes, with no disease transmission risk and consistent quality.
KEY POINTS
  • Fresh grafts risk disease transmission; lyophilization may degrade proteins
  • Microparticulate form allows uniform cryoprotectant penetration
  • Rapid thawing (~10 minutes from −80°C) for same-day use
  • Donor health screening and uniformity ensure consistent quality

Immune Privilege
Why UC tissue avoids rejection
FROM THE MANUSCRIPT

The lack of adverse events has putatively been attributed to the primitive nature of the tissue and immune privilege that is also recognized in human and equine stem cells. WJ has been successfully used in over 180 homologous sites as tissue supplement grafts in humans, showing significant improvements in pain and function scores without reported adverse events.

ⓘ WHAT THIS MEANS
Umbilical cord tissue has a special property called "immune privilege" — the body doesn't recognize it as foreign and doesn't mount a rejection response. This is the same property that allows a fetus to grow without being attacked by the mother's immune system. It's why eCTM can be used as an allograft (from a donor) without adverse reactions.
KEY POINTS
  • Fetal tissue possesses inherent immune privilege
  • Over 180 successful human applications of WJ grafts without adverse events
  • Same immune privilege recognized in human and equine stem cells
  • Explains why allogeneic (donor) tissue is well-tolerated

CONCLUSIONS — VCT26005934
⭐
Overarching Conclusion
In real-world lame performance horses, a single dose of equicenta™ CTM demonstrated superior safety and effectiveness over standard-of-care biologic therapies through 26 weeks, supporting its preferred use as a cryopreserved, ready-to-use ortho-biologic for joint, ligament, and tendon conditions. To the authors' knowledge, this represents the first and strongest level of evidence for an allograft musculoskeletal connective tissue therapy in animals.

Clinical Effectiveness

1
equicenta™ CTM produced faster and greater cumulative improvement in pain, swelling, and lameness than SOC. Significance was reached by Week 2 for pain (p<0.05) and swelling (p<0.03), by Week 4 for lameness (p<0.02), and sustained through 12 weeks for all clinical outcomes (p<0.003).
In plain terms: Horses treated with eCTM started feeling better within 2 weeks and kept improving. By 12 weeks, they were significantly better across the board compared to horses that got the other biologics.
p < 0.003 by 12 wk
2
The odds of achieving complete resolution (score of zero) by Week 12 favored eCTM over SOC — >1.6:1 for lameness, 3:1 for pain across all conditions, and 3.2:1 for joint pain specifically.
In plain terms: Horses treated with eCTM were 3 times more likely to be completely pain-free, and 3.2 times more likely to have zero joint pain, compared to horses that got standard treatments.
3.2:1 OR joint pain
3
eCTM was the only treatment that achieved both clinical and structural tissue recovery, as SOC controls improved clinically while simultaneously declining on computational ultrasound imaging.
In plain terms: The standard treatments made horses look better on the outside, but the tissue wasn't actually healing — it was getting worse on ultrasound. eCTM was the only product where the horse both felt better AND the tissue itself was repairing.

Performance Outcomes

1
Mean performance index was significantly higher for eCTM at every evaluation point (p=0.004 at 26 weeks).
In plain terms: At every single check-in over 6 months, the eCTM horses were performing at a higher level than the standard-treatment horses.
p = 0.004
2
Cumulative performance was +153% greater for eCTM (p<0.001) — the strongest p-value in the entire study.
In plain terms: When you add up all the performance gains over the entire study, eCTM horses outperformed the other group by 153%. This was the single most statistically significant finding in the trial.
p < 0.001
3
Return to prior performance favored eCTM with an odds ratio of 2.72:1 (p<0.006).
In plain terms: Horses treated with eCTM were nearly 3 times more likely to get back to the same level of competition they were at before the injury.
OR 2.72:1, p < 0.006
4
SOC joint conditions showed a declining performance pattern after Week 12, while eCTM joints continued improving (p<0.01).
In plain terms: The standard treatments started wearing off after 3 months — horses began declining. eCTM horses kept getting better. This is a critical difference for performance horses with long show seasons.
p < 0.01
5
Soft tissue conditions treated with eCTM showed sustained improvement throughout the 26-week period (p=0.21 for difference from joints).
In plain terms: eCTM worked just as well for tendons and ligaments as it did for joints — improvement held steady for the full 6 months.
p = 0.21

In plain terms: No other equine biologic product has ever shown this level of safety in a proper controlled study. eCTM is the safest option with real data behind it.

Why eCTM Works
Three-part mechanism of action from fetal umbilical tissue
The durable clinical influence of a single eCTM injection is consistent with three known features: abundant fetal proteins delivered directly to the injury site, biodegradable tissue microparticles for sustained local release, and a fibrillar tissue scaffold supporting cellular ingrowth and compressive resistance.

Durable Single-Dose Effect
Sustained improvement from one injection through 26 weeks
A single 1.5 mL injection of eCTM produced superior cumulative improvements in lameness, pain, and swelling through 12 weeks that translated into better performance at 26 weeks, particularly for soft tissue injuries. A marked post-week-12 divergence in soft tissue performance was supported by higher tissue ultrasound scores.

Superiority Over Hemoderivatives
Why autologous blood products (PRP, ACS, APS) fall short
Autologous hemoderivatives are prepared from blood that is approximately 90–92% water. Albumin and globulin comprise ~93% of soluble proteins and lack tissue scaffold or regenerative properties. Benefits are expected to be short-lived because soluble proteins are absorbed through lymphatics or degraded. SOC controls — over 85% of which received hemoderivative injections — no longer differed from baseline by 26 weeks.

Joint Safety Advantage
Favorable adverse event profile compared with other biologics
Adverse reactions from intra-articular hemoderivatives are generally considered low (<5%), yet survey data revealed that 56%, 59%, 42%, and 20% of equine specialists reported negative effects after MSCs, PRP, ACS, and APS, respectively. In a human controlled trial, PRP produced significantly more adverse events — predominantly joint flares — than plasma or saline.

Single vs. Multi-Injection Protocols
Risk-benefit of one eCTM dose versus repeated hemoderivative injections
Multiple-injection protocols for ACS and PRP are common and were prescribed in this study. In humans, a recent PRP review found no meaningful effect from a single knee injection, suggesting up to five may be required. Any protocol relying on multiple joint injections increases infection risk. In horses, three ACS injections failed to show benefit and produced an infection on the third injection.

Rehabilitation & Return to Work
Consistent rehab protocols and remarkable performance comebacks
Rehabilitation recommendations were remarkably consistent across veterinarians: early hand-walking for ~1 month, progression to swimming and ridden walking by weeks 4–12, and discipline-specific training after week 12. Significantly greater numbers of eCTM horses returned to prior level or better (8 to 1 ratio vs SOC). Several achieved world-champion performances.

Computational Ultrasound & TRS
Objective tissue remodeling measurement beyond human perception
Quantitative and computational ultrasound analyses were central to assessing soft tissue change over time. These techniques interrogated the entire imaged field at resolutions beyond human visual perception. Tissue Response Scoring (TRS) detected microstructural changes invisible to standard imaging, distinguished eCTM from SOC, and identified clinically occult non-responders.

Practical Advantages
Same-day availability, cost savings, and operational efficiency
Practical advantages include cryopreserved, same-day availability with rapid thawing (~10 minutes from −80°C). Single-dose treatment saves veterinarian and technician time and cost, lessens risks from repeated procedures, and reduces horse and client stress from repeated visits, hauling, and injections compared to multi-injection hemoderivative protocols.

Primary limitations include the open-label design, unbalanced sample sizes, and the early predictive threshold for TRS requiring prospective validation. Open-label design is inherent and common in large multi-provider clinical trials for ethical and practical reasons. Multiple bias-mitigation strategies were employed.

Product Identification & Description

equicenta™ CTM is a cryopreserved allogeneic equine umbilical cord derived connective tissue matrix (Wharton’s jelly) suspended in a cryopreservation solution. The allograft is recovered, processed, and packaged using controlled, aseptic procedures in accordance with FDA CVM GFI #218, #253, and #254. No terminal sterilization is applied.

Intended Use

equicenta™ CTM is intended for use as a cushioning, space-filling connective tissue matrix within and around equine joints, tendons, and ligaments, providing supplemental structural support to soft-tissue spaces. Intended for intra-articular, intra-lesional, or peri-lesional injection in a single equine patient on a single occasion by licensed veterinarians only.

Packaging / Storage

Each unit of equicenta™ CTM contains 1.5 mL of cryopreserved equine umbilical cord tissue allograft in a sealed inner container (cryovial), placed in a protective pouch and/or outer box. The contents of the cryovial are processed aseptically; the outer packaging and outer vial surface are non-sterile.

  • 1 Foil Pouch

Store at −40 °C or colder until use. The product is frozen. Upon receipt, promptly transfer to a freezer that maintains the recommended storage temperature. Once thawed, use immediately and do not refreeze. Do not use after the printed expiration date.

Characterization & Safety

equicenta™ CTM is a microparticulate matrix derived from equine umbilical cord (Wharton’s jelly). As with all allogeneic tissue products, potential risks include local inflammatory responses (e.g., pain, swelling, heat, transient synovitis, or effusion); immune or hypersensitivity reactions (including graft-versus-host-type or allergic responses); and a potential risk of infectious disease transmission.

Proteomic analysis identified 2,000+ proteins, including fibrillar collagens and glycosaminoglycans. The product exhibits a low inflammatory cytokine profile.

Potential Adverse Events & Reporting
  • Local inflammatory responses at or near the injection site (e.g., pain, swelling, heat).
  • Transient synovitis or effusion in the treated joint, which in controlled studies has been observed as a self-limiting transudative synovitis resolving within several days.
  • Infection at the injection or implantation site.
  • Immune or hypersensitivity reactions, including graft-versus-host-type or allergic responses.

Zoetis
Pro-Stride APS
Products
  • Pro-Stride APS (dual-centrifuge APS)
  • Restigen PRP
  • CenTrate BMA
Strengths
  • Largest animal health company ($9.5B revenue); dominant distribution (Covetrus, MWI)
  • Bertone 2014 RCT (n=40); AAEP $100K scholarship
  • No withholding period
Weaknesses
  • Autologous only — no off-the-shelf option
  • Variable patient biology affects output
  • Best evidence is for mild-moderate OA only
  • Premium pricing with capital equipment barrier ($2,500–$3,500 centrifuge lock-in)
EPL Differentiator
eCTM trial is 3.5× larger (138 vs 40 horses), tested against real SOC biologics (not saline), showed sustained improvement to 26 weeks (vs 14 days), and is off-the-shelf (no centrifuge, no blood draw, no processing variability). Same lead author (Bertone) chose to run the eCTM trial. eCTM horses were 3× more likely to be pain-free (p<0.001) and 3.2× more likely to achieve zero joint pain (p<0.004). The SOC arm — which included APS products — had 8.3× more joint adverse events than eCTM (p<0.013). ProStride's pivotal trial never measured zero-score achievement or joint-specific adverse events against a real biologic comparator.

Weaknesses
  • Orthokine requires 18–24 hour incubation — significant inconvenience

The SOC arm had 8.3× more joint adverse events than eCTM (p<0.013). A multi-injection series with higher joint adverse event risk vs. a single injection that is 8.3× safer with proven zero-pain outcomes: the choice is data-driven.
